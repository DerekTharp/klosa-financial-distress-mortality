# Reference Audit

Date: 2026-03-25

Audited manuscript: `/Users/derektharp/Documents/_Projects/26.21 Korean Financial Distress -- Mortality/submission_ssm/manuscript.md`

Scope: This audit covers citation-linked claims in the main manuscript. Repeated claims are combined into one row when the wording is materially the same in multiple places. The goal is source fidelity: does the cited source actually say what the manuscript says it says?

## Headline summary

- Total citation-linked claim rows audited: 23
- Supported and fair: 12
- Partially supported / fair but inferential: 6
- Not supported by the cited source: 4
- Could not fully verify from available source text: 1

## Highest-risk source-fidelity problems

1. `Lee and Huang` is currently overstated. The cited paper does **not** show that declining economic satisfaction trajectories predicted mortality; it says decreased self-perceived economic satisfaction was **not** significantly associated with increased mortality.
2. `Jeon and Kwon 2017` does **not** support the manuscript's current `obligatory supporter criterion` explanation or the `supplements to family support rather than replacements` phrasing.
3. `OECD Pensions 2025` does **not** appear to support the sentence about the Basic Pension being introduced in 2008, expanded in 2014, and having modest benefit levels.
4. The personality-bias paragraph needs tightening: `Jokela 2013`, `Steel 2008`, and `Gale 2017` are all being stretched beyond what they directly support.
